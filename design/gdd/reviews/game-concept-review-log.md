Summary: First formal review of the Hearthfall concept. Foundation judged sound (pitch,
pillars, scope tiering, visual anchor all strong); the cross-cutting flaw was that the
concept named desired *outcomes* without the *mechanisms* that force them. Three concept-level
blockers — (1) build/venture were additive, not a true loop; (2) the dominant Discovery+Narrative
aesthetic was unvalidated while the pitch/scope is sized for it; (3) the central mystery had no
designed payoff. All resolved in-session via targeted additions: a Loop Coupling & Economic Drain
subsection (3 interlocking home↔expedition demands + scaling-upkeep sink), a committed Central
Mystery direction (cataclysm / fallen civilization), an additive hands-on→directive decision, a
strengthened MVP that tests the sink + an escalation ramp + the expedition dependency, a
colorblind hard constraint for the art bible, and Spatial Adjacency/Zoning promoted to a candidate
core mechanic. Specialist overreach (trade-demand dynamics, expedition faucet economics, resource
counts, tuning-knob naming, cold-reentry/failure UX) deferred to the relevant GDDs.
Creative-director's path forward: this revision + a Tier-0.5 narrative/discovery prototype before
`/map-systems`.
